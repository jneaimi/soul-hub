## Outdoor Activities
- **Evening corniche stroll along JBR or Dubai Marina** — At 22C with a breeze, it's perfect walking weather after the daytime heat. The partly cloudy sky keeps things comfortable without rain risk.
- **Rooftop dining with skyline views** — The mild nighttime temperature and gentle cloud cover create an ideal atmosphere for an al fresco dinner. Wind is moderate so lighter items on the table stay put.
- **Night cycling on the Al Qudra track** — 20C feels-like temperature is ideal for cycling. The wind at 24 km/h gives a refreshing push without being disruptive on a bike.
- **Desert stargazing drive** — Partly cloudy means some stars will peek through. The cool evening air (feels like 20C) makes sitting outside in the desert genuinely pleasant.
- **Late-night paddleboarding at Kite Beach** — The wind is moderate but manageable close to shore, and the mild temperature makes being near the water comfortable without a wetsuit.

## Indoor Activities
- **Browse the exhibits at Alserkal Avenue galleries** — A culturally rich evening activity that pairs well with the cool night. Many galleries host late openings and events.
- **Catch a late showing at a luxury cinema (Reel Cinemas, VOX)** — Windy gusts outside make a plush cinema seat especially appealing. Dubai's premium cinema experiences are worth the visit.
- **Indoor rock climbing at CLYMB Abu Dhabi or Aventura Parks** — Burn energy in a controlled environment while the gusty winds make some outdoor sports less predictable.
- **Explore Dubai Mall's aquarium and underwater zoo** — A relaxed evening wander through one of the world's largest suspended aquariums — no weather dependency at all.

## Food & Drink
- **Hot karak chai from a streetside cafeteria** — The 20C feels-like temperature with a breeze makes a warm cup of spiced tea the quintessential Dubai evening comfort drink.
- **Grilled seafood at a beachside restaurant in La Mer** — Mild evening air, partly cloudy skies, and the sound of waves. Order the hammour or prawns and let the breeze set the mood.
- **Shawarma run through Deira's old souks** — Cool enough to walk the narrow souk streets comfortably. The wind won't reach you between the buildings, and the shawarma is always better from the originals.
- **Dessert at a rooftop lounge — kunafa or luqaimat** — Sweet treats with Arabic coffee on a breezy rooftop. The temperature is just right for lingering outside without overheating.

## Wellness & Relaxation
- **Outdoor yoga session in a sheltered park (Zabeel Park or Safa Park)** — At 22C with moderate wind, find a tree-sheltered spot for a grounding evening practice. The partly cloudy sky keeps it serene.
- **Traditional hammam spa experience** — Step into the warmth of a Moroccan-style hammam while the breezy evening waits outside. The contrast between the steamy indoors and cool night air is revitalizing.
- **Meditation walk along the Dubai Water Canal boardwalk** — The rhythmic sound of water combined with the 20C breeze creates natural white noise for a mindful evening walk.
- **Float therapy or sensory deprivation session** — Wind and clouds disappear entirely when you're floating in a zero-gravity pod. Emerge refreshed into the cool night.

## What to Wear
- **Light layers are key** — A breathable long-sleeve shirt or light jacket over a tee handles the 20C feels-like temperature and wind gusts comfortably.
- **Closed-toe sneakers or loafers** — Good for walking the corniche, souks, or parks without sand blowing into open sandals on gusty stretches.

## What to Bring
- **A light windbreaker or scarf** — Gusts up to 41 km/h can feel chilly at night, especially near the coast. A packable layer keeps the wind from cutting through.
- **Reusable water bottle** — Even at 22C, Dubai's low-level humidity and wind can dehydrate you faster than expected during outdoor activities.
